Color & Light in Boutique Hospitality: An Impressionist Approach

This comprehensive Bachelor's thesis research explores how Impressionist art principles can revolutionize boutique hotel design. Drawing from the artistic techniques of Monet, Renoir, and other Impressionist masters, the study investigates how color and light can be used as primary architectural elements to create emotionally resonant spatial experiences.

The research focuses on Isfahan, Iran—a UNESCO World Heritage site renowned for its architectural marvels. The proposed boutique hotel design merges architectural design with artistic sensibility, specifically targeting tourists and art enthusiasts seeking immersive cultural experiences.

Key Research Questions:

  • How can Impressionist principles of color and light be translated into architectural space?
  • What emotional and perceptual qualities can be evoked through Impressionist-inspired design?
  • How can boutique hotels serve as platforms for art-architecture integration?
  • What design strategies optimize both functional efficiency and emotional resonance?

Methodology:

The research employs a mixed-methods approach including theoretical studies on boutique hotels and Impressionism, comprehensive case studies of successful boutique hotels in Iran and internationally, and practical design exploration through spatial planning, lighting strategies, and color scheme development.

Color & Light in Boutique Hospitality: An Impressionist Approach

A Comprehensive Bachelor's Thesis Research on Integrating Impressionist Art Principles into Contemporary Hotel Design in Isfahan
January 2021 12 minutes Academic Research Isfahan, Iran Faculty of Art, Architecture and Urban Planning Bachelor of Interior Architecture Dr. Razieh Rafiee
Impressionism Boutique Hotel Color Theory Lighting Design Isfahan Spatial Atmosphere Academic

Abstract

This thesis proposes an innovative approach to boutique hotel design in Isfahan, Iran, by integrating principles from Impressionist art with a specialized focus on color and light as primary architectural elements. The project aims to create a unique hospitality experience that merges architectural design with artistic sensibility, specifically targeting tourists and art enthusiasts seeking immersive cultural experiences.

"The hotel is designed to reflect the transient effects of light and color characteristic of Impressionism, while responding to the rich cultural and historical context of Isfahan—a UNESCO World Heritage site renowned for its architectural marvels."

The research methodology includes comprehensive theoretical studies on boutique hotels, Impressionism, lighting design psychology, and extensive case studies of successful boutique hotels both in Iran and internationally. The final design proposal integrates sophisticated spatial planning, innovative lighting strategies, and carefully curated color schemes to evoke the emotional and perceptual qualities characteristic of Impressionist art.

Theoretical Framework

Boutique Hotels: Concept and Characteristics

A boutique hotel is defined by several key characteristics:

  • Small Scale: Typically under 100 rooms, allowing for personalized service
  • Unique Thematic Identity: Each hotel tells a distinct story through its design
  • Cultural Authenticity: Strong connection to local history and traditions
  • Prime Urban Locations: Often situated in culturally significant areas
  • Architectural Significance: Emphasis on interior design as a primary attraction

Impressionism in Architectural Context

Impressionism, originating in 19th-century France, revolutionized artistic expression by emphasizing:

  • Capturing fleeting moments and transient light effects
  • Use of pure, unmixed colors applied in short brushstrokes
  • Painting en plein air (outdoors) to capture natural light
  • Focus on perception and sensory experience over precise representation

Conclusion and Contribution

This thesis demonstrates the significant potential for translating Impressionist art principles into architectural and interior design practice, creating boutique hotels that are both functionally efficient and emotionally resonant. The proposed "Boutique Hotel of Color and Light" in Isfahan offers an innovative model for thematic hospitality spaces where art, architecture, and human experience intersect meaningfully.

"The project contributes to multiple fields: interior design through its innovative approach to color and light; cultural tourism by enhancing visitor experience through artistic immersion; and sustainable architecture by emphasizing perceptual richness and contextual sensitivity as alternatives to resource-intensive solutions."

This research establishes a framework for future investigations into art-architecture integration, suggesting that emotional and perceptual dimensions of space deserve equal consideration alongside functional requirements in hospitality design.
