Design Philosophy

Interior architecture transcends mere decorationit's the art of shaping human experience through space. Our approach integrates rigorous academic research from Politecnico di Milano with practical application, focusing on how spatial design influences perception, emotion, and behavior.

We believe in evidence-based design where every decisionfrom material selection to lighting strategyis informed by principles of spatial psychology, color theory, and sustainable practice. This methodology ensures environments that are not only aesthetically compelling but also enhance wellbeing and functionality.

Specializing in transitional spaces and adaptive reuse, we transform underutilized areas into meaningful environments that serve communities while respecting ecological balance and cultural context.

Sustainable Hospitality

Workshop Hospitality

A sustainable hospitality workshop in Xishuangbanna, Yunnan, blending eco-conscious design with authentic cultural immersion.

Project Vision

This project integrates traditional Dai architecture with modern sustainability principles, creating a serene green environment where visitors engage with local tea cultivation and environmental stewardship.

The design celebrates the harmony between built environment and nature, using local materials and passive design strategies to create a truly immersive cultural experience.

Design Approach

Our methodology combines vernacular architectural wisdom with contemporary sustainable technologies. Each space is designed to foster connectionbetween people, culture, and the natural environment.

Sustainability Principles

Sustainability:
Our project is built on sustainability principles, incorporating natural materials and ingredients for lasting durability. The self-sufficiency in our construction methods not only enhances the longevity of the structures but also aligns seamlessly with our commitment to environmental responsibility.

Embrace a harmonious blend of natural elements and conscientious design for an eco-friendly and enduring experience.

  • Natural local materials
  • Passive cooling systems
  • Rainwater harvesting
  • Solar energy integration
  • Waste management systems

Cultural Immersion & Target Audience

Target:
Our establishment warmly welcomes visitors seeking a serene green environment. Immerse yourself in a unique experience as you stay with us, participating in activities such as planting, harvesting the high quality local tea and contributing to the maintenance of our pristine surroundings.

Your presence will not only enrich your own journey but also contribute to the sustainable ethos of our community.

Location:
Xishuangbanna is an autonomous prefecture in China's southwestern Yunnan province, bordering Myanmar and Laos. It's known for Dai culture, distinctive temples and tropical rainforests. Prefectural capital, Jinghong, offers palm-lined streets, and the Tropical Flower and Plants Garden with a large number of fruit trees and blossoms.

Environmental Context & Climate

The site features Shorea wangtianshuea, a large species of tree in the family Dipterocarpaceae, native to southern China and in northern Laos and Vietnam. It is threatened by habitat loss. The species is under first-class national protection in China.

This natural context informs our design approach to preserve and integrate with the existing ecosystem while creating minimal environmental impact.
