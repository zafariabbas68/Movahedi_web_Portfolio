Design Philosophy

Interior architecture transcends mere decoration—it's the art of shaping human experience through space. Our approach integrates rigorous academic research from Politecnico di Milano with practical application, focusing on how spatial design influences perception, emotion, and behavior.

We believe in evidence-based design where every decision—from material selection to lighting strategy—is informed by principles of spatial psychology, color theory, and sustainable practice. This methodology ensures environments that are not only aesthetically compelling but also enhance wellbeing and functionality.

Specializing in transitional spaces and adaptive reuse, we transform underutilized areas into meaningful environments that serve communities while respecting ecological balance and cultural context.

Sustainability

Eco-Conscious Design Principles

Our project is built on sustainability principles, incorporating natural materials and ingredients for lasting durability. The self-sufficiency in our construction methods not only enhances the longevity of the structures but also aligns seamlessly with our commitment to environmental responsibility.

Embrace a harmonious blend of natural elements and conscientious design for an eco-friendly and enduring experience.

  • Natural local materials
  • Passive cooling systems
  • Rainwater harvesting
  • Solar energy integration
  • Waste management systems

Location

Xishuangbanna, Yunnan

Xishuangbanna is an autonomous prefecture in China's southwestern Yunnan province, bordering Myanmar and Laos. It's known for Dai culture, distinctive temples and tropical rainforests. Prefectural capital, Jinghong, offers palm-lined streets, and the Tropical Flower and Plants Garden with a large number of fruit trees and blossoms.

The city's Mengle Buddhist temple is a vast Dai religious complex completed in 2007, showcasing the rich cultural heritage that inspires our design.
